Natanael Cano

"Yo soy el que canta corridos tumbados"

Natanael Cano is a Mexican singer and songwriter, born on March 27, 2001 in Hermosillo, Sonora. He is known for being one of the pioneers of the genre known as "corridos tumbados," which fuses traditional corridos with elements of trap and reggaeton. He gained popularity with his single "Soy El Diablo," which went viral on streaming platforms and social media. Since then, he has released several successful albums and singles, establishing himself as a prominent figure in urban music. His songs often address themes of love, heartbreak, and life in the world of drug trafficking, which has resonated with a young audience. Natanael Cano has collaborated with several well-known artists, such as Bad Bunny and Junior H, and has been nominated for numerous awards, including the Latin Grammys. His authentic style and ability to connect with his audience have made him one of the most influential artists of the new wave of Mexican music. Additionally, his defiant attitude and focus on fashion have established him as a cultural icon in contemporary Latin music.
